Central Government Act
Section 46 in The Trade Marks Act, 1999
46\. Proposed use of trade mark by company to be formed, etc.—
(1) No application for the registration of a trade mark in respect of any
goods or services shall be refused nor shall permission for such registration
be withheld, on the ground only that it appears that the applicant does not
use or propose to use the trade mark if the Registrar is satisfied that—
(a) a company is about to be formed and registered under the Companies Act,
1956 (1 of 1956) and that the applicant intends to assign the trade mark to
that company with a view to the use thereof in relation to those goods or
services by the company, or
(b) the proprietor intends it to be used by a person, as a registered user
after the registration of the trade mark.

mark registered under the powers conferred by this sub-section, as if for the
reference, in clause (a) of sub-section (1) of that section, to the intention
on the part of an applicant for registration that a trade mark should be used
by him there were substituted a reference to the intention on his part that it
should be used by the company or registered user concerned.
